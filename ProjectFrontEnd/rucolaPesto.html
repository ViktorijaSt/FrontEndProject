And now let's cook! Because we need to be synchronized with spring - my choice is something green.
My directions to happiness is Rucola pesto! Hope all of you have try rocket, rukola or arugula - Is the same plant with many names. Rucola, (standard Italian word) is a perfect description of its spicy flavor. It has that unique "peppery" and slightly spicy taste. My mom have bed of rocola in the garden at the moment - nice job mom :) :) Fresh is the best because the taste and smell will be most potent.
Rucola goes well with parmesan cheese, tomato, is a nice addition to salads, pasta, bruschetta, on top of a pizza. Is seriously delicious. Can stand alone in a salad with a vinaigrette dressing.
It does not taste anything like Rucola. Can be a perfect sandwich meal - warm bread, parmesan and a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil. Enough for me!

I'm a pesto friend. Any kind of pesto is lord of the sauce. Very easy to make and is delicious.
Can be stored in a jar for later use. Rucola pesto is thick, creamy and rich in flavor. I'm used lately rucola pesto with spaghetti.
Giving it a unique taste and color. For this pesto you will need food processor
or blender, parmesan cheese, clove of garlic, salt, walnuts, olive oil and rucola. This pesto can be used with pizza, pasta, salad, grilled meat ...depends from your imaginations!


RECIPE

Rucola pesto

  • 6 cups fresh rucola
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (I was using black volcano salt)
  • 1 clove garlic (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons walnuts (40 grams)
  • 1/2 cup + 1-2 tablespoons olive oil
  • pinch of black pepper

    • Directions

    In a food processor or blender add the rucola, parmesan cheese, salt, chopped garlic, walnuts and half of a olive oil to start. Blend ingredients on medium speed until it starts to combine, adding from the rest of olive oil (a little at a time) and blend again for approximately 50 seconds. Blend until become creamy and smoothy. Mix with cooked spaghetti, sprinkle with chopped toasted almonds. Enjoy.
